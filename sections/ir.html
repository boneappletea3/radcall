Anticoagulation Periprocedural Guidelines
See your institution's specific protocols. The below is a general framework based on SIR guidelines.
Risk CategoryExamplesAnticoagulation
Low bleeding riskDialysis access, venography, IVC filter, PICC, central lineNo need to hold anticoagulation typically
Moderate bleeding riskCore biopsy (superficial), thoracentesis, paracentesis, drain placementIndividualize; check INR/platelet; hold anticoagulants per SIR
High bleeding riskOrgan biopsy (renal, liver), RF ablation, biliary drainageHold anticoagulation; INR <1.5; platelets >50K
SIR Thresholds (general guidance): INR <1.5 · Platelets >50,000 (moderate) / >100,000 (high risk) · Heparin: hold 4–6h; LMWH: hold 24h; Warfarin: hold 5 days; DOACs: hold 24–48h (renal-dependent for dabigatran)
