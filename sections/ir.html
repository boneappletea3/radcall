Penicillin Allergy Note: True IgE-mediated PCN allergy is uncommon (<1–2%). Skin testing or allergy referral is recommended when possible. See cross-reactivity section below for β-lactam guidance. Vancomycin is reserved for documented MRSA risk or severe PCN allergy — routine use contributes to resistance.
β-Lactam Cross-Reactivity & Graded Challenge
Northwestern Medicine β-Lactam cross-reactivity reference. Cross-reactivity is driven by R1 side-chain similarity, not the core β-lactam ring. True cross-reactivity between penicillins and cephalosporins is ~1–2%, not the historically quoted 10%. Cefazolin has a unique side chain and is generally safe in amoxicillin/ampicillin-allergic patients.
