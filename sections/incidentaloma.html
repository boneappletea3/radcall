General principle: These guidelines apply to incidentally detected findings in patients without known malignancy, immunosuppression, or signs/symptoms referable to the finding. When the patient has a known malignancy or is immunocompromised, a lower threshold for workup is appropriate. Always correlate with clinical context.

Postmenopausal Women

Key principle: Postmenopausal ovaries should be atrophic. Any new lesion warrants lower-threshold evaluation. Simple cysts ≤1 cm are near-universal incidentals and require no follow-up. Size thresholds for action are substantially lower than for premenopausal women.

Adrenal Mass
Source: ACR Incidental Findings Committee — Mayo-Smith WW et al. JACR 2017. Key principle: most incidental adrenal masses are benign adenomas. The primary goal is to distinguish adenoma from malignancy (primary adrenocortical carcinoma or metastasis) and from functioning lesions (pheochromocytoma, Conn's, Cushing's).

Chest & Mediastinum Incidentals
Source: ACR Incidental Findings Committee — Munden RF et al. JACR 2018 & 2021. Covers incidentally detected mediastinal masses, lymph nodes, pericardial findings, and lung parenchymal findings (separate from Fleischner Society pulmonary nodule guidelines, which apply to nodules ≥35 years).

Mediastinal Masses

FindingRecommendation
Anterior mediastinal mass <3 cm, smooth, homogeneous (likely thymic cyst or small thymoma)CT chest with contrast in 3 months, then annually × 2 if stable; surgical referral if >3 cm or heterogeneous
Anterior mediastinal mass ≥3 cm or heterogeneous / irregularCT with contrast; surgical or oncology referral
Middle mediastinal mass (lymph node enlargement, bronchogenic cyst)CT with contrast for characterization; if isolated bronchogenic cyst <3 cm and typical → no f/u; if atypical or >3 cm → surgical referral
Posterior mediastinal mass (neurogenic, vertebral origin)MRI spine/chest for characterization; surgical referral for most neurogenic tumors
Thymic cyst (<3 cm, homogeneous, non-enhancing)No follow-up if <3 cm and clearly cystic

Pancreatic Cysts
Source: ACR Incidental Findings Committee — Megibow AJ et al. JACR 2017. Most incidental pancreatic cysts are benign (serous cystadenoma, pseudocyst) or low-grade malignant potential (IPMN). Key distinction: main duct vs. branch duct involvement. High-risk stigmata warrant GI/surgical referral.
High-risk stigmata (always refer to GI/surgery): Main pancreatic duct ≥10 mm · Obstructive jaundice with pancreatic head lesion · Mural nodule with enhancement · Suspicious solid component · Positive cytology · Abrupt MPD caliber change with distal parenchymal atrophy.

Pituitary Incidentaloma
Source: ACR Incidental Findings Committee — Hoang JK et al. JACR 2018. Most incidental pituitary findings are microadenomas (nonfunctioning). Key questions: size, mass effect on optic chiasm, and hormonal activity. Endocrinology referral is the standard for macroadenomas.
ModalitySize / FindingRecommendation
CT (incidental)Any pituitary lesionDedicated pituitary MRI (3T preferred) for characterization; distinguish from normal variant
MRI — microadenoma (<10 mm)<6 mmNo follow-up imaging; endocrinology only if clinical concern for hormonal excess
MRI — microadenoma6–9 mmRepeat pituitary MRI in 12 months; if stable → discontinue; endocrinology referral
MRI — macroadenoma (≥10 mm)10–29 mm, no chiasm contactEndocrinology referral; repeat MRI in 6 months; ophthalmology if any visual symptoms
MRI — macroadenoma≥30 mm or chiasm contact / compressionUrgent endocrinology + neurosurgery referral; visual field testing
FDG-PET (incidental uptake)Any focal pituitary uptakeDedicated pituitary MRI; endocrinology referral
Biochemical screen for all macroadenomas: Prolactin (rule out prolactinoma — medical Rx first), cortisol/ACTH (Cushing's), IGF-1 (acromegaly), thyroid function, LH/FSH/testosterone or estradiol.
📄Hoang JK et al. ACR Incidental Pituitary Findings. JACR. 2018;15(7):966-972.

Thyroid Nodules
Source: ACR Incidental Findings Committee — Hoang JK et al. JACR 2015 (CT/MRI/PET incidentals). For dedicated thyroid ultrasound, use ACR TI-RADS. These recommendations apply specifically to thyroid nodules found incidentally on non-thyroid imaging (CT, MRI, PET).

Incidental on CT or MRI

FindingRecommendation
Single nodule <1 cm, no suspicious features (smooth margins, no calcification, homogeneous)No further workup
Single nodule 1–1.5 cm OR multiple nodules <1.5 cm, no suspicious featuresThyroid ultrasound if clinically appropriate; endocrinology referral optional
Any nodule ≥1.5 cmThyroid ultrasound and endocrinology referral
Suspicious features on CT/MRI (irregular margins, microcalcifications, taller-than-wide, extracapsular extension)Thyroid ultrasound regardless of size; endocrinology referral
Diffuse thyroid enlargement / multinodular goiter without dominant noduleThyroid function tests; ultrasound if clinical concern

Incidental on FDG-PET

FindingRecommendation
Focal FDG uptake in thyroidThyroid ultrasound + endocrinology referral — malignancy risk ~30–35% for focal uptake
Diffuse FDG uptakeThyroid function tests — likely thyroiditis; imaging follow-up not routinely required
ACR TI-RADS for dedicated thyroid ultrasound: uses composition, echogenicity, shape, margin, and echogenic foci to assign points (TR1–TR5) with size-dependent biopsy thresholds. ACR TI-RADS Calculator
